Tim Berners-Lee

An English computer scientist who created the World Wide Web (WWW), which dramatically changed the way information is shared globally. He was born on June 8, 1955, in London, England. Tim developed the first web browser, the HTTP protocol, and the HTML markup language, which became the foundation of the modern Internet.

important dates in life

  • 1955

    Born on June 8 in London to a family of mathematicians who worked on the UK's first commercial computer, the Ferranti Mark 1.

  • 1976

    Graduated from Oxford University, where he studied physics. During his student years, he built his first computer using a television, a processor, and various electronic components.

  • 1980

    Started working at CERN as an independent contractor. There, he created a system for storing and sharing information, which he called Enquire (a precursor to the future WWW).

  • 1989

    Presented the concept of the World Wide Web in his work "Information Management: A Proposal." This year, his idea was approved.

  • 1990

    Developed the first web browser, WorldWideWeb, and the first web server, httpd. In December, the first website was launched at info.cern.ch.

  • 1993

    The WWW officially became accessible to all users worldwide. Tim decided not to patent his technology to ensure that the Internet would remain free.

  • 1994

    Founded the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C), an organization responsible for developing web standards.

  • 2004

    Received a knighthood from Queen Elizabeth II for his contributions to global society.

  • 2012

    Actively supported open access to the Internet, speaking out against censorship and privacy violations on the Web.

  • 2021

    Berners-Lee sold the source code of the World Wide Web as an NFT for $5.4 million. He donated the proceeds to charity.

Interesting Facts

  • The first website, info.cern.ch, was dedicated to describing how the World Wide Web works.

  • His inventions transformed communication, the economy, education, and social connections worldwide.

  • Tim actively promotes the concept of a decentralized Internet to prevent technology monopolization by large corporations.
